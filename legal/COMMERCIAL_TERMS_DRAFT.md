# Commercial Terms Draft (OEM / White-Label)

Last updated: 2026-04-25

This is business-language draft text for attorney review, not legal advice.

## Defined terms

- **Agreement**: The governing commercial agreement and any incorporated order form/addendum.
- **Customer**: The purchasing entity receiving commercial rights.
- **Circaevum Technology**: Software, APIs, SDKs, and hosted services provided by Circaevum.
- **Product**: Customer's application or service that incorporates Circaevum Technology.

## 1) License grant (draft)

Subject to fees and compliance with this Agreement, Circaevum grants Customer a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the Circaevum software and services for Customer's internal business operations and authorized product integrations.

## 2) Attribution and branding (default)

Customer will display `Powered by Circaevum` in the user interface where Circaevum rendering is exposed to end users, unless a White-Label Addendum is executed.

## 3) White-label option (paid addendum)

If Customer purchases White-Label Rights:

- Customer may remove the default attribution overlay.
- Customer may apply approved alternative branding treatment.
- Trademark use remains subject to explicit written approval.

## 4) Restrictions

Customer will not:

- Misrepresent origin of Circaevum technology.
- Remove legal notices from source distributions where required.
- Use Circaevum Marks outside approved trademark permissions.

## 5) Open-source component compliance

Customer acknowledges the software may include third-party open-source components and agrees to comply with applicable license notices and obligations.

## 6) Support and updates

Support scope, response targets, update cadence, and maintenance windows are defined in the applicable order form or support exhibit.

## 7) Fees and audit (optional section)

License fees may be metered by usage, seats, instances, or contract tier.  
Customer will maintain reasonable records for usage-based billing verification.

## 8) Termination effects

Upon termination, Customer will cease use of commercial-only materials unless otherwise stated in a surviving license grant.

## 9) IP ownership

Circaevum retains ownership of Circaevum IP and associated trademarks.  
Customer retains ownership of Customer data and Customer-specific content.
