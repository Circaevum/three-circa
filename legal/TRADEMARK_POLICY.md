# Circaevum Trademark Policy (Draft)

Last updated: 2026-04-25

## Purpose

This policy defines permitted and restricted use of the Circaevum Marks.

## Defined terms

- **Circaevum Marks**: "Circaevum", associated logos, trade dress, and other brand identifiers used by Circaevum.
- **Licensee**: Any person or entity using Circaevum software or services under a valid license or agreement.

## 1) Allowed nominative use

Licensees may use Circaevum Marks in factual statements such as:

- "Built with Circaevum"
- "Powered by Circaevum"
- "Compatible with Circaevum"

Conditions:

- Use must be truthful and non-misleading.
- Use must not imply endorsement, sponsorship, or affiliation unless expressly authorized.

## 2) Prohibited use without written permission

- Using Circaevum logos in product names, icons, or primary product branding.
- Registering confusingly similar domains, package names, or social handles.
- Presenting Products in a way that suggests official Circaevum ownership or endorsement.
- Using Circaevum Marks after rights are revoked or expired.

## 3) Co-branding and white-label

Co-branding, reseller, and white-label branding rights require signed commercial terms that explicitly grant those rights.

## 4) Brand quality and misuse

Circaevum may request corrective action for uses that:

- Create customer confusion.
- Dilute or tarnish the Circaevum brand.
- Conflict with agreed attribution or commercial terms.

## 5) Revocation

Trademark permissions may be suspended or revoked for non-compliance.

## 6) Software license separation

Software license rights and trademark rights are separate.  
A software license does not by itself grant broad rights to use Circaevum Marks.
