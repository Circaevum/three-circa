# Circaevum Attribution Policy (Draft)

Last updated: 2026-04-25

## Purpose

This policy sets default attribution requirements for Products that incorporate or expose Circaevum Technology.

## Defined terms

- **Circaevum Technology**: Circaevum-provided rendering components, libraries, hosted services, or related SDK functionality.
- **Product**: Any application, website, service, or experience operated by a Licensee that uses Circaevum Technology.
- **Licensee**: Any person or entity using Circaevum Technology under an open-source license, commercial agreement, or other permitted grant.

## 1) Required attribution text

Unless expressly waived in writing, each public-facing Product must display:

`Powered by Circaevum`

Recommended destination for linked attribution:

- `https://circaevum.com`

## 2) Display requirements

Attribution must be:

- Continuously visible during normal Product use.
- Legible on common desktop and mobile viewport sizes.
- Not hidden behind low-discoverability interaction states.

Acceptable placements include:

- Persistent bottom-left or bottom-right overlay within the rendered interface.
- Persistent footer attribution where overlays are not used.

## 3) Visibility standards

- Use readable typography and sufficient visual contrast.
- Do not clip, mask, blur, or reduce opacity to make attribution effectively unreadable.
- Do not style attribution in a way likely to confuse users about source identity.

## 4) Scope

This attribution policy applies by default to:

- Public hosted products and demos.
- Customer-facing internal tools used in commercial/marketing contexts.
- White-label or OEM deployments unless superseded by a signed waiver/addendum.

## 5) Waiver path

Circaevum may grant written exceptions, including:

- No-attribution white-label rights.
- Alternative wording.
- Co-branding layouts.

Any exception must be documented in a signed commercial agreement.

## 6) Relationship to license and trademark

Attribution requirements are typically enforced through a combination of contract and trademark policy.  
Applicability to any given deployment depends on the governing license and agreement set for that deployment.
